Interstellar Review

Jonathan Joseph 10/23/2025

If a character from Street Fighter such as Ryu were to encounter Jin Kazama  from Tekken,  Ryu might be able to put up a fight if it wasn't for the fact that Jin  exists within the 3rd dimension.  As humans, we are 3-dimensional creatures can move forward (x plane), upward (y plane), and sideways (z plane) plane.  Logically Ryu  wouldn't be able to perceive Jin  since beings cannot perceive dimensions greater than their own. In Albert Einstein's theory of relativity, time is characterized as the 4th dimension, something we humans cannot perceive. In Interstellar, one of the main protagonists goes into the black hole Gargantua and interacts with the path, which essentially sets the story in motion.

Interstellar is a story which takes place in the year 2067 and humanity is on its last leg because there was a global blight which killed most crops. The main protagonist, Joseph A. Cooper, was a NASA pilot but is now a farmer because the population is so in need of food.  Although I didn't fully understand the plot of Interstellar I really did like the movie,  it was the only piece of media I have ever cried to, during the scence of Cooper watching time fly past him, and his daughter become the same one who was 10 years old when he left on his mission,  turn 33 years old. I think my favorite charecter in the fill would be TARS, at first I thought he was going to turn evil, but I started to like "him" more and more as the movie went on.
